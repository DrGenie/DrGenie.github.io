# Design and content audit

## Main issues addressed

- The homepage contained too many visually equal cards and calls to action, which weakened hierarchy.
- The navigation exposed too many top-level links.
- The profile portrait was visually dominant relative to the academic positioning statement.
- Publications were presented as a very long fully expanded page.
- The tools page included a non-live STEPS placeholder.
- Several content areas used card grids when simple lists were more readable.
- The public blog included an editorial-planning page and malformed category metadata on two posts.
- Repeated publication counts and homepage publication displays were hard-coded rather than generated from the publication data.
- Generic featured-publication pages lacked verified article-specific summaries and were removed rather than retained as filler.

## Final design direction

The revised design is intentionally restrained: a white institutional header, compact circular portrait, blue accent, strong typography, thin rules instead of shadows, and list-based presentation for publications, projects, courses, supervision and talks. The homepage retains all essential audiences and content while reducing the number of separate blocks and decorative elements.
