# Editorial calendar

This editorial calendar records planned topics rather than publishing thin placeholder posts. Each future post should begin with a concrete decision problem, define the standard economic benchmark, introduce the behavioural mechanism, distinguish descriptive evidence from policy judgment and link to related teaching or research pages.

Planned topics include the Ellsberg paradox and ambiguity aversion in health technology decisions; defaults in superannuation, organ donation and preventive health; present bias and delayed beneficial health actions; framing survival, mortality and treatment benefit; status quo bias in health plans and technology adoption; anchoring in prices and willingness-to-pay questions; choice overload in treatment decisions; social norms and public-health behaviour; and what eye tracking adds to discrete choice experiments.
