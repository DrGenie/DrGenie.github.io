# Content simplification report (Phase 2)

The goal of this pass was a calmer, more scannable, more informative site, achieved through hierarchy rather than by deleting substance. Detail was moved to internal pages; landing pages were shortened.

## Navigation
- Reduced to seven primary items: Home, Research, Publications, Tools, Teaching & Supervision, Writing & Talks, About.
- CV moved to a right-hand utility link; Contact moved to the footer and hero secondary link.
- Grants moved under Research and the CV; Conferences moved under Writing & Talks; Blog and the standalone Talks page folded into Writing & Talks.
- Dropdown menus removed in favour of flat landing pages, so every resource is reachable within two clicks.

## Homepage
- Reduced to seven calm sections: hero, research focus, selected work, research translated into decisions, teaching and supervision, featured writing and talk, contact.
- Removed the decorative metric strip, including the "countries of appointment" indicator and the empty citation and h-index tiles.
- Removed the large tool card grid, the repeated publication grid, and the embedded YouTube player (replaced by a lightweight link).
- Removed the hero social-icon row (profiles remain in the footer, About, and Contact) and reduced calls to action to three primary actions plus one secondary link.
- Selected work now shows exactly three items (one project, one paper, one tool), each with a one-line explanation of question, method, and contribution.

## Research
- Research is now a concise hub: a short intellectual overview, three themes, three selected projects, and links to Research impact, Data & code, Grants, and Publications. Full grant records and collaborator lists were moved off the landing page.

## Tools
- The Tools landing is now a short three-row list; full detail lives on each tool page.

## Blog
- Categories reduced to six canonical themes. The placeholder post "Starting to write here" was retired.

## Footer
- Reduced to name and institution, contact, a few profile links, CV, and copyright, with no repeated primary navigation.
